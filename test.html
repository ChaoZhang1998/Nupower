introduction


Since the fall of the Fukushima nuclear power plant, discussions on nuclear power have been controversial. The development of power industry in China has advanced by leaps and bounds, and nuclear power plays a key role in it. At this stage, thermal power generation is the most important form of power generation in China, even in the whole world. On the contrary, technology in nuclear power is only mastered by dozens of countries.

In the process of China's power development, how does the importance of thermal power and nuclear power change? What is the difference in people’s attitudes between thermal power and nuclear power? How does the "nuclear" gradually become a necessary source of energy for the supply and demand of thousands of households? What are the differences between the United States, Japan and China in these areas?

As a student majoring in Electric Engineering, I am curious about these questions. Afterwards, I will analyze the development of China's power industry from a unique perspective, and extract the key role of thermal power and nuclear power; then, I will further explore the meaning of "nuclear energy" in the international community, especially among the three countries - China, the United State and Japan. Of course, I will give the results of above questions to visualize, and ultimately expect to draw some interesting conclusions.



福岛核电站泄漏以来，有关核电的讨论一直争议不断。中国电力产业的发展突飞猛进，核电在其中起着关键作用。现阶段，火力发电是我国乃至整个世界最主要的发电形式，与之相反，核电技术却仅仅被几十个国家所掌握。

在我国电力发展进程中，火电和核电的重要程度是如何变迁的呢？人们对火电和核电的态度有什么不同呢？“核”是如何从人人闻风丧胆的武器逐渐变成现在为千家万户供需能量所必不可少的来源呢？美国、日本与中国又在这些方面有哪些不同呢？

我作为一名电气专业的学生，对这些问题总是心怀好奇。接下来，我会分析我国电力产业的发展，并从中提取火电和核电的关键性作用，与美国、日本进行比较，进一步探索“核能”意符在国际社会中的变迁，同时赋予分析结果以可视化呈现，最终期望得到一些有意思的结论。
